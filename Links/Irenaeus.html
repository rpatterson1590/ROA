Beliefs Irenaeus Rejected, Found in Modern Heretical Groups

Posted on July 1, 2025 • Category: Apologetics

1. Polytheism / Multiple Gods

Found in: LDS (Mormons)

Irenaeus taught:

There is one true God, eternal and uncreated, who created all things. Against Heresies, Book I, ch. 22:

"There is but one God, the Father, and one Christ Jesus, our Lord"

Polycarp taught:

There is one true God, and Jesus Christ is His Son and our Savior. Letter to the Philippians, ch. 3 & Martyrdom of Polycarp, ch. 9:

“Away with the atheists!” (referring to the polytheistic Roman crowd)
"Whosoever does not confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh is antichrist…"

They rejected:

Gnostic ideas of many divine emanations—this lines up against Mormonism’s belief in many gods and humans becoming gods.

2. Jesus as a Created Being (Not Fully God)

Found in: Jehovah’s Witnesses, some Hebrew Israelites

Irenaeus taught:

Christ is eternally divine, uncreated, and equal with the Father. Book III, ch. 19:

"He is Himself in His own right... God, and Lord, and King eternal."

He rejected:

Any teaching that Jesus was merely an exalted creature or a lesser god.

3. Modalism / Oneness Theology

Found in: Oneness Pentecostals, Modalists

Irenaeus taught: The Trinity

Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as distinct persons but one God. Book IV, ch. 20:

"The Father is indeed above all, and He is the Head of Christ... and the Word was made flesh..."

He rejected:

The idea that the Father, Son, and Spirit are the same person with different "modes."

4. Secret Knowledge / Special Revelation

Found in: LDS (extra scriptures), JW (Watchtower), SDA (Ellen G. White visions), British Israelites (hidden identity doctrines)

Irenaeus taught:

The apostolic teaching is public, sufficient, and passed down clearly. Book I, ch. 10:

“The Church... received from the apostles... one faith, and one God.”

He rejected:

Hidden or elite knowledge as heretical—like what Gnostics claimed.

5. Non-Eternal Hell / Soul Sleep

Found in: Jehovah’s Witnesses, SDA

Irenaeus taught:

The soul is immortal, and punishment for the wicked is eternal. Book V, ch. 27:

“The lost shall be punished forever... they do not cease to exist.”

He rejected:

Annihilationism or soul sleep.

6. Strict Torah Keeping / Sabbatarian Legalism

Found in: SDA, Hebrew Israelites

Irenaeus taught:

Christ fulfilled the Law, and Christians are not bound by the Old Covenant. Book IV, ch. 16:

“Christ did not abrogate the Law, but fulfilled it... now we are under the new covenant.”

He rejected:

Legalistic return to Mosaic laws and Sabbath-keeping as requirements for salvation.

7. Anglo/British Israelism (Ethnic Exclusivity)

Found in: British Israelites, some Hebrew Israelite sects

Irenaeus taught:

The gospel is for all nations, and the true Israel is spiritual, not racial. Book III, ch. 21:

“God calls from the Gentiles a people for His name…”

He rejected:

The idea that salvation was based on ethnicity or lineage.

8. Denial of Bodily Resurrection

Found in: Some Gnostic-influenced cults, esoteric New Age

Irenaeus taught:

A literal, physical resurrection of the body. Book V, ch. 7:

"The resurrection of the just takes place... in the body.”

He rejected:

The idea that resurrection is merely spiritual or symbolic.
